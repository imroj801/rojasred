Miguel Rojas

I currently work as a DevOps engineer, relishing hikes with my family, flying drones (I own a Mavic 3 Pro), and indulging in kayaking. Born in Peru, I was brought to the U.S. by my parents at the age of seven. My tech journey began at seventeen when I applied for DACA, sparking my passion for technology. My career kicked off at GeekSquad as a technical support agent. The job was a mix of love and hate, encountering things on people's computers that'd spare them nightmares. Keen on advancing to home support, I aimed for that but landed at Intermountain HealthCare as a helpdesk analyst. During this tenure, I crafted a tool using JQUERY, Vanilla JavaScript, HTML, and BootStrap. Despite our IT department's reluctance to host the site, I managed it through the FILE EXPLORER! Yes, not a typo—the yellow folder in your taskbar. Running it locally on an internal network, I streamlined our helpdesk operations, integrating our call queue and essential tools. Despite the success, I thirsted for more knowledge. The journey into engineering began then. In 2015-2016, unaware that coding could be a career, I applied for a quality engineering position. Zero confidence, yet I pushed through the interview, thinking they humored a naive kid. Surprisingly, I received an offer, starting with a modest 50k annually (feeling rich!). Three months in, faced with organizational changes, panic struck—I'd just made an offer on a house! Concerned about potential layoffs, I began hunting for a new job. Fortunately, my manager, navigating similar paths, landed a managerial role and rehired me at Mountain America Credit Union. Starting as a QA engineer, progressing to an SDET, then transitioning to a DevOps role, I later earned a promotion to DevOps Engineer. Everything seemed on track, but my tendency to seek new challenges led me to create a blog to stay engaged.
